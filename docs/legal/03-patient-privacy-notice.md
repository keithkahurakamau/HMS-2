## 1. What information we collect

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identification | Name, date of birth, sex, national ID, passport, residence, photo on file |
| Contact | Telephone, postal address, email, next-of-kin contact |
| Demographic | Marital status, occupation, religion, nationality, primary language |
| Insurance / payer | NHIF / SHIF number, scheme name, member number |
| Clinical | Symptoms, diagnoses, vitals, medication, laboratory results, imaging, allergies, chronic conditions, mental-health notes, HIV status, obstetric and reproductive-health information |
| Genetic / biometric | Where collected, with separate consent |
| Financial | Invoices, payments, M-Pesa references |
| Audit | Records of who accessed your information and when |

Some of this information is **"sensitive personal data"** under the Kenya
Data Protection Act, 2019, particularly your health, genetic, biometric, and
HIV-status data.

## 2. Where we get it

- Directly from you when you register, fill in forms, or speak with our staff.
- From the next-of-kin you designate.
- From other registered health facilities and laboratories that refer you to us, or to whom we refer you (with your consent).
- From your insurance scheme where you have authorised that exchange.
- From our medical devices when used during your care.

---

## 3. Why we use it (purposes) and the lawful basis

| Purpose | Lawful basis under KDPA |
|---|---|
| Provide medical care to you (diagnosis, treatment, follow-up) | s30(b) — preventive or occupational medicine; s30(c) — medical diagnosis and treatment; consent obtained on registration |
| Administer your visits, queues, appointments | Performance of contract / legitimate interest |
| Bill you or your insurance scheme | Performance of contract |
| Comply with reporting duties (e.g. notifiable diseases, KMPDC obligations) | Legal obligation (s30(g)) |
| Quality improvement and clinical audit (using de-identified data where possible) | Legitimate interest |
| Research, training, statistics (only with separate consent or properly anonymised) | Explicit consent under s30(a); or anonymisation |
| Communicate with you (appointment reminders, lab-result availability) | Consent or legitimate interest |
| Protect our staff, you, and other patients (safeguarding) | Vital interests (s30(d)) / public interest |

## 4. Who we share it with

We share your information only when necessary for your care, when you ask us
to, or when the law allows or requires it. This includes:

- **Other healthcare providers** involved in your care, on a need-to-know basis (e.g. consulting specialist, laboratory, pharmacy, ambulance).
- **The Ministry of Health and the KMPDC** where notification is legally required.
- **Your insurance / NHIF / SHIF** for claim processing, with your authorisation.
- **MediFleet (HMS-2 Operator)**, which hosts and operates the software platform we use, as our Data Processor under a written contract.
- **Sub-processors** of MediFleet listed in their Sub-processor Register (available on request) — these are technical service providers like hosting and payment gateways. They process your data only on documented instructions and under written contract.
- **Courts, regulators, or law-enforcement** where lawfully compelled.
- **Your designated representative or next-of-kin** in emergencies or as you have authorised.

We do **not** sell your information.

---

## 5. Where your information is stored

Your information is stored in databases hosted by reputable providers,
including providers based outside Kenya. Where data is hosted outside Kenya,
the transfer is covered by appropriate safeguards under KDPA s48–49
(standard contractual clauses or comparable measures). You can ask the DPO
for the list of countries and safeguards.

---

## 6. How long we keep it

| Record type | Retention |
|---|---|
| Adult medical records | Minimum **10 years** after your last contact with us (KMPDC retention rule) |
| Paediatric records | Minimum **25 years** after the patient's date of birth (or 10 years after last contact, whichever is longer) |
| Mental-health records | Per the Mental Health Act and KMPDC guidance |
| Maternity records | Minimum **25 years** |
| Audit logs of access to your record | Minimum **6 years** |
| Billing records | Minimum **7 years** (tax law) |

After the retention period, records are anonymised or securely destroyed.
The full retention schedule is set out in our internal Retention Schedule
(available on request).

## 7. Your rights under the KDPA

You have the right to:

| Right | What it means | KDPA section |
|---|---|---|
| Be informed | Know we hold data on you and what we do with it | s26(a), s28 |
| Access | Get a copy of your information | s26(b), s39 |
| Correction | Ask us to correct inaccurate information | s26(c), s40 |
| Erasure ("right to be forgotten") | Ask us to delete your information, subject to legal retention | s26(e), s40 |
| Object | Object to a specific processing activity | s26(d) |
| Restrict | Ask us to stop or limit certain processing while a query is resolved | s26(d), s40 |
| Portability | Get a structured, machine-readable copy of data you provided | s26(f) |
| Withdraw consent | Withdraw consent you previously gave, without affecting prior processing | s30(1) proviso |
| Lodge a complaint | Complain to us first; if unresolved, to the ODPC | s56 |

**How to exercise your rights.** Speak to any of our staff, contact the DPO
(details above), or use the in-portal "Request my data" form. We will respond
**within thirty (30) days** (extendable by a further sixty (60) days for
complex requests, with reasons given). There is no fee for the first request
in any twelve-month period.

**If you are unhappy with our response**, you can complain to the Office of
the Data Protection Commissioner:

- Website: https://www.odpc.go.ke
- Email: info@odpc.go.ke

## 8. Children and vulnerable persons

For patients under eighteen (18) years of age, consent is provided by a
parent or legal guardian. Where appropriate, the views of children aged
twelve (12) and above are also sought. Heightened protections apply per
KDPA s33.

---

## 9. Automated decision-making

We do not currently use any solely automated decision-making that produces
legal effects concerning you. Some clinical-decision-support features may
suggest possible diagnoses or medication-conflict alerts, but a qualified
clinician always reviews and decides.

---

## 10. Security

We use technical and organisational measures to protect your information,
including:

- Encrypted transmission between your device and our systems.
- Role-based access control — only authorised staff can see your record, and only the parts they need for your care.
- Logging of every access to and change in your record (we can show you who looked at your record).
- Strong authentication for staff accounts.

No system is 100% secure. We test our defences and our software supplier's
defences regularly. If a breach occurs that is likely to result in a high
risk to you, we will notify you per KDPA s43.
