# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

> **Status:** DRAFT v0.1
> **Audience:** Anyone using the HMS-2 / MediFleet Platform, the Patient Portal, the public website, or any MediFleet-operated API.
> **Where it appears:** stable URL on the MediFleet website; linked from the Platform sign-in and the Patient Portal sign-up.

We provide HMS-2 / MediFleet for the lawful operation of hospitals and the
self-service of their patients. This Acceptable Use Policy describes uses
that are **not allowed** and the consequences of violating it.

---

## 1. Application

This AUP applies to:

- **Hospital Authorised Users** (clinical and administrative staff)
- **Patients** using the Patient Portal
- **Visitors** to the MediFleet public website
- **Anyone interacting with our APIs** via tooling, scripts, or integrations
- **Sub-contractors and integrators** acting on behalf of any of the above

You agree to this AUP by using the Platform.

---

## 2. Prohibited content and conduct

You must not use the Platform or any MediFleet service to:

### 2.1 Violate Kenyan or applicable foreign law
- Process patient data without lawful basis under the KDPA.
- Practise medicine without registration with the KMPDC (or relevant board).
- Operate a pharmacy or laboratory function without the appropriate licence.
- Make a payment that is part of a money-laundering, terrorism-financing, or sanctions-evasion scheme.
- Infringe any third party's intellectual property.

### 2.2 Compromise security
- Probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of any part of the Platform without an authorised, written engagement.
- Bypass, attempt to bypass, or weaken any access control, authentication, or rate-limiting mechanism.
- Use credentials that are not yours.
- Introduce malware, ransomware, time bombs, or destructive code.
- Use the Platform to send unsolicited bulk messages (spam) via the Messaging or Notifications modules.
- Attempt to reverse-engineer, disassemble, or extract underlying source code.

### 2.3 Misuse patient or staff information
- View a patient record you have no legitimate clinical or administrative reason to view ("snooping").
- Export, screenshot, photograph, or forward patient information to non-authorised persons or non-secure channels (personal email, WhatsApp, social media, cloud drives outside the Platform).
- Use patient information for research, training, marketing, or any non-care purpose without explicit consent and Hospital authorisation.
- Modify another user's or patient's record without authorisation.
- Impersonate another user, patient, or staff member.

### 2.4 Disrupt service
- Generate excessive load, scrape, or otherwise interfere with the Platform's normal operation.
- Probe the underlying multi-tenant architecture to access other tenants' data.
- Overwhelm the Patient Portal sign-in with automated requests.

### 2.5 Engage in abusive communication
- Use any in-Platform messaging or comment feature to harass, intimidate, threaten, or discriminate against any person.
- Post content that is unlawful, defamatory, obscene, or that breaches another person's privacy.

### 2.6 Misuse children's data
- Process the data of minors except in the patient's clinical course of care, with the consent of a parent or legal guardian.
- Direct any portal account sign-up at children under 18.

### 2.7 Misuse Platform IP
- Remove or alter any copyright, trademark, or notice.
- Build a competing service derived from access to the Platform.
- Use Platform logos or trademarks without prior written permission.

## 4. Consequences of violation

Depending on the severity, we may, in our reasonable discretion:

1. **Warn** the responsible user and the Hospital.
2. **Suspend** the user's account.
3. **Terminate** the user's account or, in the case of a Hospital-wide breach, suspend the Hospital's access pending investigation.
4. **Cooperate** with law enforcement and provide information lawfully required by court order.
5. **Pursue** civil remedies for losses, including breach of the MSA where applicable.

Reckless or malicious snooping on patient records is a serious matter under
KDPA s72 and may attract personal criminal liability in addition to
disciplinary action.

## 5. Security research disclosure

We welcome responsible security research. If you discover a vulnerability:

- **Email** **security@[PLACEHOLDER]** with a clear description and reproduction steps.
- **Do not** access, modify, or exfiltrate data beyond what is strictly required to demonstrate the issue.
- **Do not** publicly disclose details until we confirm a fix is deployed (we aim to confirm a fix within ninety (90) days of triage).
- We will acknowledge your report within five (5) business days and keep you informed of progress.
- We do not currently operate a paid bug-bounty programme but will publicly acknowledge contributors who follow this policy.
