## 2. Grant of right to use the Platform

2.1 Subject to the Customer's payment of the Fees and compliance with this
Agreement, the Operator grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable,
non-sublicensable right during the Term to access and use the Platform for
the Customer's internal healthcare operations.

2.2 The right extends to the Customer's Affiliates only if expressly listed
in the Order Form.

2.3 The Customer may permit Authorised Users to access the Platform. The
Customer remains responsible for all acts and omissions of its Authorised
Users as if they were the Customer's own acts and omissions.

2.4 **Restrictions.** The Customer shall not, and shall procure that its
Authorised Users do not:

  (a) reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Platform;
  (b) attempt to derive the source code or underlying algorithms of the Platform;
  (c) use the Platform to provide a competing service to third parties;
  (d) circumvent any access control, throttling, or security measure of the Platform;
  (e) use the Platform in violation of any applicable law, including the KDPA,
       the Health Act 2017, the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018, or
       any direction issued by the ODPC, KMPDC, or other regulator;
  (f) upload malware or material that infringes third-party rights.

## 3. The Operator's obligations

3.1 **Provision of the Platform.** The Operator shall provide the Platform
in accordance with the Subscription Plan, the Service Level Agreement (Document 11),
and applicable law.

3.2 **Security.** The Operator shall maintain appropriate technical and
organisational measures ("TOMs") to protect Customer Data, as described in
Schedule 1 of the DPA.

3.3 **Updates.** The Operator may update the Platform from time to time.
Material changes that materially reduce functionality shall be notified to the
Customer at least thirty (30) days in advance and shall not take effect during
the Customer's current paid period without the Customer's consent.

3.4 **Support.** The Operator shall provide support per the SLA (Document 11).

3.5 **Personnel.** The Operator's staff with access to Customer Data shall be
subject to written confidentiality undertakings and KDPA training.

---

## 4. The Customer's obligations

4.1 **Lawful basis.** The Customer warrants that it has a lawful basis under
the KDPA (Sections 30–32) for every category of Customer Data it processes
through the Platform.

4.2 **Patient notices and consent.** The Customer shall issue patients with a
Patient Privacy Notice (template in Document 03) and obtain signed consent
where required (template in Document 08) before entering their data into the
Platform.

4.3 **Account security.** The Customer shall:

  (a) keep account credentials confidential;
  (b) require strong passwords and, where supported, enable multi-factor authentication;
  (c) notify the Operator promptly of any suspected unauthorised access;
  (d) ensure Authorised Users complete the Customer's KDPA training before being granted access.

4.4 **Permitted use.** The Customer shall use the Platform only for the
purpose of providing healthcare services and the ordinary administrative,
billing, and reporting activities of a hospital.

4.5 **Co-operation.** The Customer shall provide the Operator with reasonable
co-operation in investigating any security incident or data subject request.

## 5. Fees and payment

5.1 The Customer shall pay the Fees set out in the Order Form.

5.2 Unless the Order Form states otherwise, Fees are payable monthly in advance
and are due within fourteen (14) days of the invoice date.

5.3 Fees are exclusive of VAT and any other applicable taxes. VAT is payable
at the rate applicable in Kenya at the tax point of supply.

5.4 **Late payment.** Undisputed Fees more than thirty (30) days overdue
attract interest at the Central Bank of Kenya base rate plus four percent (4%)
per annum, accruing daily.

5.5 **Suspension.** The Operator may suspend the Platform if any undisputed
invoice is more than forty-five (45) days overdue, after giving fourteen (14)
days' written notice. Suspension shall not prejudice the Customer's data
export rights under Clause 12.

5.6 **Annual indexation.** Fees may be increased once per calendar year by no
more than the official Kenya National Bureau of Statistics Consumer Price
Index ("CPI") plus three percent (3%). Notice of any increase shall be given
at least sixty (60) days before the effective date.

## 6. Data protection

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that with respect to Patient Personal Data, the
Customer is the **Data Controller** and the Operator is a **Data Processor**
within the meaning of the KDPA.

6.2 The processor obligations of the Operator are set out in the **Data
Processing Agreement (Document 02)**, which is incorporated by reference and
forms an integral part of this Agreement. In the event of conflict between
this Agreement and the DPA on a data-protection matter, the DPA prevails.

6.3 The Customer authorises the Operator to engage the sub-processors listed
in the Sub-processor Register (Document 15). Material changes to that list
shall be notified per the DPA.

## 7. Intellectual property

7.1 **Platform IP.** The Platform, including all underlying software, design,
trademarks, and documentation, is and remains the property of the Operator
(or its licensors). No right is granted to the Customer other than the right
to use the Platform during the Term.

7.2 **Customer Data.** Customer Data remains the property of the Customer
(or, where applicable, of the data subject). The Operator's only right in
Customer Data is the limited right to process it as a Processor for the
purposes of providing the Platform.

7.3 **Feedback.** Any feedback or suggestions the Customer voluntarily provides
about the Platform may be used by the Operator without restriction, without
attribution, and without payment of royalties.

7.4 **Anonymised data.** The Operator may create and use aggregated,
de-identified statistical data derived from Customer Data for the purpose of
operating, improving, and benchmarking the Platform, provided that:

  (a) the data is anonymised in a manner that cannot be re-identified to the
       Customer or any data subject; and
  (b) such use does not breach KDPA s2 or the ODPC Guidance on Anonymisation.

## 8. Confidentiality

8.1 Each Party shall keep the other Party's Confidential Information
confidential and use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

8.2 Confidential Information may be disclosed:

  (a) to Affiliates, employees, professional advisers, sub-processors, and
       auditors on a need-to-know basis under equivalent confidentiality
       undertakings;
  (b) where required by law or by a regulator (in which case the disclosing
       Party shall give the other Party reasonable prior notice where lawful
       to do so).

8.3 The confidentiality obligations survive termination for a period of five
(5) years, save that obligations relating to Patient Personal Data continue
for as long as the Operator holds such data.

  (a) it has the right to grant the rights granted by this Agreement;
  (b) the Platform, as delivered, will materially conform to its published

9.3 Except as expressly set out, all other warranties, conditions, and
representations are excluded to the maximum extent permitted by law.

10.1 Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits a Party's liability for:

  (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
  (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
  (c) breach of confidentiality;
  (d) any liability that cannot be lawfully excluded;
  (e) the Operator's wilful breach of its obligations under the DPA;
  (f) the Customer's failure to pay Fees.

10.2 Subject to Clause 10.1, neither Party shall be liable for any:

  (a) loss of profits, revenue, business, or anticipated savings;
  (b) loss or corruption of data, except to the extent caused by the Operator's
       breach of the DPA or the TOMs;

10.3 Subject to Clauses 10.1 and 10.2, each Party's total aggregate liability
under or in connection with this Agreement in any rolling twelve (12) month
period shall not exceed an amount equal to **the Fees paid by the Customer in
the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the liability**,

Clause 6 / the DPA in respect of regulatory fines lawfully imposed on the

## 11. Term and termination

11.1 **Initial term.** The Agreement begins on the Effective Date and
continues for the period set out in the Order Form (the **"Initial Term"**).

11.2 **Renewal.** Thereafter the Agreement renews for successive twelve (12)
month periods (each a **"Renewal Term"**) unless either Party gives the other
not less than sixty (60) days' written notice of non-renewal before the end of
the then-current term.

11.3 **Termination for cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement
immediately on written notice if the other Party:

  (a) commits a material breach not cured within thirty (30) days of written
       notice (or, for non-payment, within fourteen (14) days);
  (b) becomes insolvent, enters administration, has a receiver appointed, or
       passes a winding-up resolution.

11.4 **Termination by Customer for regulatory cause.** The Customer may
terminate immediately if the Operator's continued operation would cause the
Customer to be in unlawful breach of the KDPA, the Health Act 2017, or a
directive of a competent regulator that cannot be cured within thirty (30)
days.

11.5 **Effect of termination.** On termination:

  (a) the Customer's access to the Platform shall cease;
  (b) the Operator shall, within thirty (30) days, make Customer Data
       available for export per Clause 12;
  (c) the Operator shall delete or anonymise all Customer Data per Clause 12
       and the DPA;
  (d) accrued rights and remedies are not affected.

---

## 12. Data export and deletion on termination

12.1 **Export.** Within the thirty (30) day period following termination, the
Customer may request export of its Customer Data. The Operator shall provide
a download or transfer in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
(JSON or CSV). Schema documentation will accompany the export.

12.2 **Deletion.** After the export period or earlier on the Customer's
written instruction, the Operator shall:

  (a) delete the Customer's live database within thirty (30) days;
  (b) purge backups containing Customer Data within ninety (90) days,
       subject only to retention required by Kenyan law;
  (c) provide written confirmation of deletion on request.

12.3 **Statutory retention.** Where Kenyan law (in particular KMPDC and Health
Records and Information Managers Act 2016) requires the Customer to retain
records for a defined period, the Customer must export prior to termination
and assume control. The Operator does not retain Customer Data beyond the
periods set out in Document 14 absent the Customer's instruction.

## 13. Force majeure

13.1 Neither Party is liable for delay or failure caused by events beyond its
reasonable control, including acts of God, war, civil unrest, pandemic-related
public health orders, country-wide internet outages, or actions of a
governmental authority. The affected Party shall promptly notify the other and
shall mitigate the impact.

13.2 If the force majeure event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive
days, either Party may terminate this Agreement on written notice.

## 15. Governing law and dispute resolution

15.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Republic of Kenya**.

15.2 The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith
negotiation between senior representatives within thirty (30) days.

15.3 Failing resolution under Clause 15.2, the dispute shall be referred to
**mediation** under the Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration ("NCIA")
Mediation Rules.

15.4 If mediation does not resolve the dispute within sixty (60) days of
referral, the dispute shall be finally resolved by **arbitration** under the
NCIA Arbitration Rules. The seat of arbitration shall be Nairobi. The language
of the arbitration shall be English. The arbitral tribunal shall consist of a
sole arbitrator appointed by agreement of the Parties or, failing agreement,
by the NCIA.

15.5 Either Party may seek interim relief from the Kenyan High Court in
support of arbitration.

## 16. General

16.1 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other
Party's prior written consent, except that the Operator may assign to an
Affiliate or to a successor by way of merger, sale of substantially all
assets, or reorganisation, provided the assignee assumes all obligations.

16.2 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (including the Order Form, DPA, SLA,
and other referenced documents) is the entire agreement of the Parties and
supersedes all prior representations.

16.3 **Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the rest of the
Agreement remains in effect, and the unenforceable provision shall be replaced
by an enforceable provision that most closely reflects the original intent.

16.4 **No waiver.** A failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver.

16.5 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including
by electronic signature, each of which is an original.

16.6 **Conflict order.** In case of conflict between documents, the order of
precedence is: (1) Order Form; (2) DPA; (3) this MSA; (4) SLA; (5) other
referenced documents.
