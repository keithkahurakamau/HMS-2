## 2. Account security

You undertake to:

- keep your password and any second-factor device strictly private;
- not share your account with anyone, even a colleague — every audit log
  entry is tied to your account;
- sign out when leaving a shared workstation;
- notify the Hospital's IT administrator and the Operator's security
  contact (**[PLACEHOLDER — security email]**) immediately if you suspect
  your account or your patient's account has been compromised.

The Hospital will deactivate your account when you change role, leave, or are
on extended absence.

## 3. Acceptable use

You **must**:

- access only the patient records relevant to your duties;
- treat all patient information as strictly confidential — both during and
  after your employment with the Hospital;
- enter clinical information accurately, contemporaneously, and only on the
  patient to whom it relates;
- comply with the KMPDC Code of Professional Conduct (or equivalent for your
  profession) and the Hospital's clinical governance policies.

You **must not**:

- look at the records of a patient who is not under your care without an
  authorised reason (this is logged and treated as a serious disciplinary
  matter);
- export, copy, photograph, or screenshot patient information outside of
  legitimate clinical or administrative need;
- forward patient information to personal email, messaging apps, or cloud
  drives outside the Platform;
- attempt to bypass any access control, modify URLs to access another
  Hospital's data, or otherwise interfere with the Platform's security;
- use the Platform's communications features (Messaging, Notifications) for
  abuse, harassment, or non-clinical promotion;
- introduce malware or knowingly compromise integrity;
- reverse-engineer the Platform.

## 4. Logging and monitoring

Every action you take is logged. Logs include your user ID, the action, the
record involved, your IP address, and the timestamp. These logs are used to:

- assist with clinical handover and quality improvement;
- demonstrate compliance with the KDPA (specifically the "audit trail"
  expectation in s41);
- investigate any suspected misuse or security incident.

Your Hospital may review your access at any time as part of clinical
governance. The Operator does not routinely access these logs but may do so
to:

- investigate a security incident at the request of the Hospital or the
  ODPC;
- respond to a lawful order;
- maintain or support the Platform.
