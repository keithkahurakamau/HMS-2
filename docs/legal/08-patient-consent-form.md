## 2. What you are being asked to consent to

We are asking you to consent to **two related but distinct things**. You can
agree to both, one, or neither. Refusing the second does **not** affect the
care you receive.

### A. Consent to care (Health Act 2017)

> "I, the above-named patient (or representative), have had the opportunity
> to ask questions and have understood the purpose, benefits, risks, and
> alternatives of the assessment, treatment, and procedures that I receive
> at **[PLACEHOLDER — Hospital]**. I consent to receive care at this
> hospital under the supervision of its registered clinicians."

☐ I consent to receive care at this hospital.
☐ I have read or have had read to me the Patient Privacy Notice (Document 03).
☐ Signature: __________________________ Date: ____________

### B. Consent to the processing of my health information (KDPA s30)

> "I understand that my health information is **sensitive personal data**
> under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019. The Hospital will use my
> information for my care, for billing, and for the legal duties of the
> Hospital. The Hospital uses the **HMS-2 / MediFleet software platform** to
> store and manage this information. The platform is operated by a Data
> Processor under a written contract that protects my data. Some technical
> sub-processors of the platform are located outside Kenya; appropriate
> safeguards under KDPA s48–49 apply. I have been given the contact details
> of the DPO and informed of my rights to access, correct, delete, and
> object to certain uses of my information. The Patient Privacy Notice
> describes all of this in full."

☐ I consent to the Hospital and its software platform processing my health
   information for my care and the related purposes set out in the Patient
   Privacy Notice.
☐ I understand I can withdraw this consent at any time by contacting the
   Hospital DPO. Withdrawal will not affect processing already done.

## 3. Optional, specific consents

These are **separate** consents. Each tick-box must be specifically marked
by you. Leaving a box unticked means **you have not consented** to that use.

### 3.5 Sensitive data flags

The Platform records some data with heightened protection (marked as
"sensitive" in its medical-history module): **HIV status, mental-health
information, obstetric and reproductive-health information, genetic test
results**. Care for these matters still requires consent under the
relevant statute (e.g., HIV and AIDS Prevention and Control Act 2006).

☐ I understand that the above categories have heightened protection and
   are recorded separately in my file.

### 3.6 Research and statistics
☐ I consent to my de-identified data being used for medical research
   and quality improvement statistics.
☐ I consent to being **contacted in the future** about specific research
   opportunities that I may choose to join (a separate consent will be
   sought for each study).

### 3.7 Photography and clinical images
☐ I consent to the Hospital recording clinical photographs (e.g., wound
   progression, dermatology) for the purpose of my care.
☐ I consent to such images being used, in anonymised form, for teaching
   purposes within the Hospital.

### 3.8 Audit log access
I understand that an audit log of who accesses my record is kept, and I can
request a copy from the DPO. ☐ Understood.

## 4. Your rights — summary

(See Patient Privacy Notice, Document 03, for the full list.)

You can:
- Access your data — get a copy.
- Correct inaccurate data.
- Ask for deletion (subject to KMPDC and Health Records Act retention rules).
- Object to specific processing.
- Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting prior processing.
- Complain to the Hospital DPO and, if unresolved, to the ODPC (https://www.odpc.go.ke).
