## LEGAL BASIS

Pursuant to Section 30 of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and the Children Act, Cap. 586 of the Laws of Kenya, a child under the age of eighteen (18) years cannot legally consent to medical treatment on their own behalf. This form must be completed and signed by the patient's parent or legal guardian. The guardian signing this form confirms they have lawful authority to make medical decisions on behalf of the minor named below.

This form is used in conjunction with the Patient Informed Consent for Treatment form (`CONSENT/PATIENT_TREATMENT_CONSENT.md`), which must also be completed for the specific procedure.

## RETENTION NOTICE

This document shall be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years from the date the minor patient attains the age of majority (18 years)**, in accordance with the Health Act, No. 21 of 2017 (Kenya) and the Children Act, Cap. 586. This means the document must be retained until the patient is at least 25 years old, or for 7 years from the date of treatment, whichever is later.
