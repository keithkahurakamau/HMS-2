## LEGAL BASIS

Pursuant to Section 30 of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and the common law doctrine of informed consent, this form records the voluntary, informed consent of the patient (or authorised guardian) to the proposed medical treatment described below. No treatment shall be administered without a completed and signed copy of this form except in a clinical emergency where the patient is incapacitated and no guardian is available, in which case the emergency exception under applicable law applies.

## PART E — PATIENT RIGHTS

The following rights have been explained to the patient / guardian:

1. **Right to information:** You have the right to receive full information about your condition and the proposed treatment in a language and manner you understand.
2. **Right to refuse:** You have the right to refuse treatment or any part of it at any time without penalty to your ongoing care.
3. **Right to withdraw consent:** You may withdraw this consent at any time before or during treatment by informing a member of the clinical team. Withdrawal of consent will not affect the quality of care provided to you for conditions for which you do consent to treatment.
4. **Right to a second opinion:** You may seek a second medical opinion before agreeing to this treatment.
5. **Right to privacy:** Your personal and medical information will be handled in accordance with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and the hospital's Privacy Notice.

## RETENTION NOTICE

This document shall be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years** from the date of treatment in accordance with the Health Act, No. 21 of 2017 (Kenya). Where the patient is a minor, the retention period is seven (7) years from the date the patient attains the age of majority (18 years). Clinical records are subject to pseudonymization (not deletion) upon any erasure request, consistent with the retention obligation under the Health Act 2017.
