## 4. WHO WE MAY SHARE YOUR DATA WITH

We may share your personal data with the following categories of recipients where necessary and lawful:

- **Other healthcare providers:** Hospitals, clinics, laboratories, radiology centres, and specialist clinicians involved in your care or to whom you are referred.
- **Insurance companies and NHIF:** For the purpose of processing insurance claims and benefits, where you have provided consent or where sharing is necessary for the claim you have requested.
- **National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF):** For billing and reimbursement of covered services.
- **National and county public health authorities:** Where reporting is required by law (e.g., notifiable diseases under the Public Health Act, Cap. 242).
- **The Kenya Medical Practitioners and Dentists Council (KMPDC)** and other regulatory bodies: In response to lawful regulatory enquiries.
- **Laboratory and diagnostic service providers:** Who process specimens and samples on our behalf as data processors.
- **IT service providers and cloud hosting providers:** Who provide technical infrastructure for the MediFleet system, subject to binding Data Processing Agreements.
- **Legal representatives and courts:** Where required by a court order or legal process.
- **Employers or other third parties:** Only with your explicit written consent.

We do not sell your personal data to any third party.

## 5. HOW LONG WE KEEP YOUR DATA

| Data Category | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Medical records (all clinical data) | Minimum 7 years from the date of last treatment | Health Act, No. 21 of 2017 |
| Billing and financial records | Minimum 7 years | Tax Procedures Act; financial regulations |
| Consent records | Minimum 7 years | Health Act 2017; KDPA 2019 |
| Data breach records | Permanently | KDPA 2019 S.43 |
| Minor patient records | 7 years from date of attaining majority (age 18) | Health Act 2017; Children's Act |
| Research records | Duration of study + 7 years | Ethics requirements; Health Act 2017 |

After the applicable retention period, records are securely destroyed or permanently anonymised.

## 6. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE KENYA DATA PROTECTION ACT 2019

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

| Right | KDPA Section | How to Exercise |
|---|---|---|
| **Right to be informed** — to receive this notice | S.25 | This notice satisfies this right |
| **Right of access** — to receive a copy of your personal data | S.26 | Complete a Data Subject Access Request form (available at the Records Office) |
| **Right to rectification** — to correct inaccurate data | S.27 | Submit a written request to the Records Office |
| **Right to data portability** — to receive data in a machine-readable format | S.28 | Complete a Data Portability Request form |
| **Right to object** — to object to specific processing activities | S.36 | Complete a Right to Object form |
| **Right to restriction** — to restrict how your data is processed | S.34 | Complete a Right to Restriction form |
| **Right to erasure** — to request deletion of your data | S.35 | Complete a Right to Erasure form (Note: clinical records are subject to Health Act 2017 retention requirements — see below) |

**Important note on the Right to Erasure:** The Health Act 2017 requires the hospital to retain medical records for a minimum of 7 years. Where you submit an erasure request, the hospital will pseudonymise your records (removing directly identifying information) rather than permanently deleting the underlying clinical data, to comply with this legal retention obligation. The MediFleet system implements this pseudonymisation automatically. You will be notified of the outcome of your request.

## 8. DATA SECURITY

We take the security of your personal data seriously. The MediFleet Hospital Management System implements the following security measures:

- Role-based access controls ensuring staff access only data relevant to their function
- Encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS) for all system communications
- Audit logs of all access to patient records
- Password policies enforcing minimum complexity requirements
- Automatic session timeouts (15 minutes for clinical access; 60 minutes for patient portal)
- Refresh token security controls and reuse detection
- Regular security assessments and staff training
- Physical security controls for paper records
