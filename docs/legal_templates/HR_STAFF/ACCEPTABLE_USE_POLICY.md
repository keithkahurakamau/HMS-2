## 1. PURPOSE

This policy governs the acceptable use of [HOSPITAL NAME]'s information technology systems, including the MediFleet Hospital Management System, computers, tablets, mobile devices, and network infrastructure by all staff, contractors, and volunteers.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to **all individuals** who access hospital IT systems, whether on-site or remotely:
- Permanent and temporary employees
- Contractors and consultants
- Volunteers and students on placement
- Any other authorised users

---

## 3. USER ACCOUNTS AND AUTHENTICATION

3.1 **Individual Accounts:** Each user is issued a unique login account. Accounts must not be shared with any other person under any circumstances, including colleagues, supervisors, or family members.

3.2 **Password Requirements:** All passwords must meet the following minimum standards:
- At least **8 characters**
- At least one **uppercase letter**
- At least one **lowercase letter**
- At least one **digit** (0–9)
- At least one **special character** (e.g., `!@#$%^&*(),.?":{}|<>`)

3.3 **Password Security:**
- Never write passwords down or store them unencrypted
- Never share passwords via email, chat, or phone
- Change your password immediately if you suspect it has been compromised

3.4 **First Login:** All new accounts require a mandatory password change on first login. Do not skip or defer this step.

3.5 **Session Management:**
- Lock your screen or log out when leaving a workstation unattended, even briefly
- MediFleet access tokens expire every 15 minutes — the system handles renewal automatically while you are active
- If you see the message **"Refresh token reuse detected — all sessions revoked"**, contact IT immediately as this may indicate your account has been compromised

---

## 4. PATIENT DATA ACCESS

4.1 Access patient records **only when required** for the direct performance of your duties.

4.2 Never access patient records out of personal curiosity, even for patients you know personally.

4.3 Every access to patient records in MediFleet is **logged automatically** in the audit trail. Unexplained or unauthorised access will be investigated.

4.4 Sensitive patient categories (mental health, obstetric, HIV-related records) may only be accessed by staff with explicit authorisation.

4.5 Do not disclose patient information to third parties — including the patient's family — without the patient's explicit consent or a lawful authority.

---

## 5. DATA HANDLING

5.1 **Do not export** patient data from MediFleet without written authorisation from the Hospital Administrator or Data Protection Officer.

5.2 **Do not copy** patient records to personal devices, personal email accounts, or external storage media (USB drives, external hard drives).

5.3 **Do not photograph or screenshot** patient information on your personal phone or camera.

5.4 Paper printouts of patient records must be:
- Used only for the stated clinical or administrative purpose
- Stored securely when not in use
- Shredded securely when no longer required

---

## 6. INTERNET AND EMAIL USE

6.1 Hospital computers and network access are provided for **work purposes**. Incidental personal use is permitted but must not interfere with work duties.

6.2 **Prohibited activities** on hospital systems include:
- Accessing or distributing illegal, offensive, or discriminatory content
- Online gambling or cryptocurrency mining
- Downloading or installing unauthorised software
- Using hospital systems for personal commercial activities
- Accessing personal social media during working hours using hospital computers

6.3 Be cautious of **phishing emails** — do not click suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders. Report suspicious emails to IT immediately.

---

## 7. PERSONAL DEVICES (BYOD)

7.1 Personal devices may be used to access MediFleet only if:
- Approved by the Hospital Administrator
- The device has a screen lock enabled
- The device is not shared with other household members when accessing hospital systems

7.2 If a personal device used to access hospital systems is lost or stolen, report it to IT immediately so that active sessions can be revoked.

---

## 8. INCIDENT REPORTING

Report the following to IT or the Data Protection Officer **immediately**:
- Suspected unauthorised access to your account
- "Refresh token reuse detected" messages
- Lost or stolen devices used to access hospital systems
- Suspicious emails or phishing attempts
- Any observed policy violations by colleagues

Do not attempt to investigate a suspected breach yourself.

---

## 9. CONSEQUENCES OF POLICY VIOLATION

Violation of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension of system access pending investigation
- Disciplinary action up to and including dismissal
- Civil or criminal liability under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019 and the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, 2018
