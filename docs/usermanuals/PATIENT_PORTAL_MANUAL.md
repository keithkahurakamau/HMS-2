# MediFleet — Patient Self-Service Portal Manual

**Audience**: Patients registered at a MediFleet hospital  
**System**: MediFleet Patient Portal  
**Version**: 1.0 | **Date**: 2026-05-16 | **Review**: 2027-05-16

---

## What Is the Patient Portal?

The MediFleet Patient Portal is a secure, read-only self-service area where you can view your appointment history, invoices, and medical history entries from any device with a web browser.

> **Important:** The Patient Portal is completely separate from the hospital staff login. Do not use the staff login page. Go to the portal address given to you by the hospital.

---

## Section 1: Accessing the Patient Portal

### 1.1 What You Need Before You Start

- Your **Outpatient Card Number** (OP Number) — printed on your outpatient card, e.g. `OP-2026-0045`
- Your **Date of Birth**
- The **last 4 digits** of the mobile phone number you registered with the hospital

If you do not have your OP number, contact the hospital reception.

### 1.2 Logging In

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari).
2. Navigate to the Patient Portal address provided by your hospital (e.g., `https://hospital-name.medifleet.co.ke/patient`).
3. The portal login page appears.
4. Enter your **OP Number** (e.g., `OP-2026-0045`).
5. Enter your **Date of Birth** in the format shown (DD/MM/YYYY or select from the calendar).
6. Enter the **last 4 digits** of your registered phone number.
7. Click **Verify & Sign In**.

> **Note:** The system performs a three-factor check. All three pieces of information must match what the hospital has on file. If any one is incorrect, access is denied.

### 1.3 If You Cannot Log In

| Problem | What to do |
|---|---|
| "Information does not match our records" | Check you are using the OP number from your outpatient card. Contact the hospital if your phone number has changed. |
| OP number not recognised | Contact hospital reception — your registration may be under a different number |
| Forgot your date of birth on file | Visit the hospital in person with your ID to confirm registered details |

### 1.4 Your Session

- Your portal session lasts **60 minutes**.
- After 60 minutes of inactivity, you will be logged out automatically.
- You will need to re-enter all three details to log in again.
- For security, always log out when finished, especially on shared or public devices.

---

## Section 2: Navigating the Portal

Once logged in, you will see a navigation menu with three sections:

| Section | What you can see |
|---|---|
| **Appointments** | Your scheduled and past appointments |
| **Billing** | Your invoices and payment status |
| **Medical History** | Your recorded medical history entries |

There is also a **Profile** area showing your basic registered details (name, OP number, date of birth, contact).

---

## Section 3: Viewing Your Appointments

1. Click **Appointments** in the navigation menu.
2. A list of your appointments appears, sorted by date (most recent first).
3. Each appointment shows:
   - **Date and Time**
   - **Doctor's Name**
   - **Department**
   - **Status**: Scheduled, Confirmed, Completed, Cancelled, or No-Show
   - **Notes** (if any were added at booking)

### What the statuses mean

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Scheduled | Appointment booked, not yet confirmed |
| Confirmed | Confirmed by the clinic |
| Completed | The appointment has taken place |
| Cancelled | The appointment was cancelled |
| No-Show | You did not attend the appointment |

> **Note:** You cannot book, change, or cancel appointments through the portal. Contact the hospital reception to make changes.

---

## Section 4: Viewing Your Invoices

1. Click **Billing** in the navigation menu.
2. Your invoices appear in a list.
3. Each invoice shows:
   - **Invoice Date**
   - **Invoice Reference**
   - **Total Amount (KES)**
   - **Amount Paid (KES)**
   - **Balance Due (KES)**
   - **Payment Status**: Pending, Partially Paid, Paid, or Cancelled
   - **Payment Method** (if paid): Cash, M-Pesa, Cheque

4. Click an invoice to expand the itemised breakdown showing individual charges (consultation, laboratory, pharmacy, radiology).

### Invoice statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Pending | Invoice has not yet been paid |
| Partially Paid | A payment has been made but a balance remains |
| Paid | Fully settled |
| Cancelled | Invoice was voided by the hospital |

> **Note:** You cannot make payments through the portal. Payments are processed at the hospital billing desk or via M-Pesa to the hospital's Paybill number.

---

## Section 5: Viewing Your Medical History

1. Click **Medical History** in the navigation menu.
2. Your recorded history entries appear grouped by type:
   - Surgical History
   - Family History
   - Social History
   - Immunizations
   - Allergies
   - Chronic Conditions
   - Past Medical Events
   - Obstetric History (where applicable)

3. Each entry shows: Title, Description, Date (approximate or exact), Severity, and Status (Active/Resolved/Managed).

### What you will NOT see

Certain sensitive medical records are restricted from the patient portal for privacy and clinical reasons. These include:
- Mental health records
- Records specifically marked as sensitive by your clinical team

If you need access to restricted records, speak to your doctor directly.

> **Your Rights:** Under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, you have the right to request a full copy of all your data (including restricted records). Submit a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) at the hospital reception or admin office.

---

## Section 6: Logging Out

1. Click your name or the menu icon in the top right corner.
2. Click **Log Out**.
3. Your session is ended and you are returned to the login screen.

> **Always log out** when you finish, especially on a shared computer, tablet, or phone.

---

## Section 7: Privacy and Data Security

- Your portal session uses secure HTTPS encryption.
- Your session token is stored only in your browser's secure cookies and expires after 60 minutes.
- The portal is completely **read-only** — no changes can be made to your records through the portal.
- Every time you view your medical records, the access is logged by the hospital in accordance with the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 (Section 26).

### Your Data Rights

| Right | How to exercise it |
|---|---|
| Right to access all your data (DSAR) | Visit hospital admin or reception desk |
| Right to correct inaccurate data | Request correction at reception |
| Right to restrict processing | Submit a written request to hospital administration |
| Right to erasure | Submit a written request; note that clinical records are retained for 7 years per Health Act 2017 |
| Right to complain | Contact the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC), Kenya |

---

## Section 8: Getting Help

| Issue | Who to contact |
|---|---|
| Cannot log in / OP number unknown | Hospital reception desk |
| Incorrect appointment or billing information | Hospital billing or records office |
| Medical history is incomplete or incorrect | Your treating doctor or medical records office |
| Data privacy concerns | Hospital Administrator or ODPC Kenya |
| Technical issues with the portal | Hospital IT support |
